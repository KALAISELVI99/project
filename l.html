People Also Ask About Tamil Nadu
What is famous for Tamil Nadu?
The South Indian state of Tamil Nadu is known all over the world for its ancient temples, stunning architecture, food, movies, the various folk-dance forms as well as the famous Carnatic music. additionally, Tamil Nadu is also famous for:

- Hill stations like Ooty, Yercaud, Kodaikanal, etc. - Being home to eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites - Meenakshi Amman Temple, Madurai - Kanchipuram Silk - Chettinad Cuisine - Marina Beach - Kanyakumari - Thanjavur Paintings - Rameshwaram Temple. Which is the coolest place in Tamil Nadu?
Ooty is at the top of the coolest places to visit in Tamil Nadu. Also known as The Queen of Hill Stations, Ooty is a popular tourist spot, famous for its picturesque landscapes, amazing vistas and a soothing ambience. Which are the best historical places in Tamil Nadu?
1. Madurai: Located on the banks of the Vaigai River, Madurai is one of the most historical tourist places in Tamil Nadu, in addition to being the third-largest city in the region. A famous pilgrimage place, Madurai is also often regarded as the Athens of the East.
2. Tiruchirapalli: This city has its roots deep in the history of South India. Located along the banks of the Cauvery River, the city has also had major significance during the Carnatic Wars and is a major pilgrimage centre in South India.
3. Mahabalipuram: AUNESCO World Heritage Site, this city is home to the famous Shore Temple which dates back to the 7th century. The city also served as the second capital of the Pallava kings of Kanchipuram and is amongst the best places to visit in Tamil Nadu.
4. Vellore: One of the oldest cities of South India, Vellore is also known as the Fort City of Tamil Nadu. It also has its roots in the early Dravidian culture and has been ruled by many famous kings throughout the years.
Which are the places to see in Yercaud, Tamil Nadu?
1. Yercaud Lake: This pristine lake offers spectacular views of the surrounding hills and verdant meadows. Located in the heart of the city, you can also enjoy delicious local snacks from the nearby cafes and food stalls or enjoy boating in the lake.
2. 32-km Loop Road: This scenic road takes you through the different attractions of Yercaud, starting from the picturesque Yercaud Lake and through the scenic coffee and tea plantations.
3. Pagoda Point: This is one of the most serene places in Yercaud, and offers a calm solitary ambience to the tourists. In addition to offering stunning views of Yercaud and the verdant meadows and valleys, it is also a great place to enjoy hiking.
4. Bears Cave: This cave is said to have been created by bears and has served as a secret hideout place of Tipu Sultan. It is also a great place for adventure enthusiasts, who can explore a certain section of the cave for some thrill.
Which are the religious places in Tamil Nadu?
1. Kanchipuram: This city has been ruled by various powerful dynasties throughout the ages and is home to several ancient temples and structures. Some temples that you can visit here include the Kailasanathar Temple, Varadaraja Temple, etc.
2. Rameswaram: This holy place is also mentioned in the Ramayana and is said to be the place where Lord Rama built a bridge to Sri Lanka. From Agnitheertham to Ramanathaswamy Temple, there are various important religious sites scattered across this city.
3. Thanjavur: In addition to being the home of the famous Thanjavur style of paintings, this city also has the Great Living Chola Temples, which are also considered to be UNESCO World Heritage Monuments.
4. Madurai: Situated along the Vaigai River, this holy city has many temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. You can also find stunning ancient temples such as the Vandiyur Mariamman Temple, Sri Meenakshi Temple, the Narasingam Yoga Narasimha Perumal Temple and others here.
